Groups can be used with text substitution commands or macros in MAGIC mode, to duplicate parts of the target into the result.

In the search string, a group is defined as a portion beginning by the characters backlash and opening parenthesis "\(" and ended by the characters backlash and closing parenthesis "\)". There can be up to nine such groups.

In the replace string, groups appear as a backlash followed by a decimal digit ("\1" to "\9"). The portion of the target string matched by the nth group is substituted to each occurrence of \n to form the replacement string.

The function &group n can be used in macros to obtain the text matched by the nth group in a search.
